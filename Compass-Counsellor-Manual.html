Welcome to Compass Counsellor

Your trusted read-only dashboard for monitoring connected students' college application progress.

What is Compass Counsellor?

Compass Counsellor is a secure, read-only dashboard designed exclusively for school counsellors. It enables you to monitor the college application progress of students who have consented to share their information with you through the Compass student application.

Unlike traditional access to student files, Compass Counsellor provides:

  • Real-time visibility into student readiness scores and application milestones
  • Structured insights across five key dimensions: language, academics, activities, essays, and milestones
  • Privacy-first design with explicit student consent and immediate revocation on disconnect
  • Compliance by default with FERPA, GDPR, COPPA, PDPA, and other global regulations
📋 What's the Connection to Compass?

Compass is a student-facing application that helps learners track and prepare for college applications. When a student invites you as a counsellor in Compass and you accept, you gain access to their Compass Counsellor dashboard—but only to the extent they've authorized. Students can revoke access instantly at any time.

Your Role as a Counsellor

As a school counsellor using Compass Counsellor, you are an authorized processor of education records. This means:

  • You access student data for a legitimate educational interest: supporting college application readiness
  • You have a duty of confidentiality and cannot re-disclose data to unauthorized parties
  • You must follow applicable data protection laws in your jurisdiction
  • You are responsible for security of your account and must not share access with others

Key Principles

✨ Read-Only Access

You cannot modify, delete, or export student data from Compass Counsellor. All changes to student records must be made directly in Compass by the student.

✨ Student Consent Required

Students must actively invite you and accept your connection request. Access is never granted by default. Every student connection is voluntary.

⚠️ Immediate Revocation

Students can disconnect from you at any time. When they do, your access is revoked immediately and permanently until they re-invite you. This is a core safeguarding principle.

📋 Data Protection is Your Responsibility

You are a data processor under FERPA, GDPR, PDPA, and other frameworks. You must handle student data securely, maintain strong passwords, log out on shared devices, and report breaches within 72 hours to compliance@sailedu.co.

Getting Started with Compass Counsellor

Set up your account and understand your dashboard in five steps.

Sign Up

To create your Compass Counsellor account:

  1. Navigate to Compass Counsellor login page and select "Sign Up"
  2. Enter your email address (use your school domain if available)
  3. Provide your full name as you wish it to appear to students
  4. Enter your school or organisation name
  5. Create a strong password (minimum 8 characters, mix of upper/lowercase, numbers, symbols)
  6. Review and accept the Data Obligations modal (see Data Obligations section for full details)
  7. Confirm your email address via the verification link sent to your inbox
  8. Log in with your credentials
💡 Email Verification

Check your spam folder if you don't see the verification email within 5 minutes. Resend the email from the sign-up page.

Data Obligations Acceptance

During sign-up, you must review and accept a modal summarizing your data protection responsibilities. You can re-read this modal at any time from Settings → Data Obligations.

⚠️ Mandatory Review

This is not optional. By accepting, you confirm you understand your role as a data processor under FERPA, GDPR, COPPA, PDPA, PIPEDA, and other applicable laws. See the Data Obligations section for comprehensive details.

Connecting to Students

Invite students to share their application progress with you through secure, consent-based requests.

Sending a Connection Request

To connect with a student, follow these steps:

  1. Navigate to the "Requests" tab in your dashboard
  2. Click "Send Connection Request"
  3. Enter the student's email address (the email they used to create their Compass account)
  4. Review the validation checks (the system confirms the email is registered in Compass)
  5. Click "Send Request"
  6. Wait for the student to accept (you'll see status updates in real-time)
💡 Pro Tip

You can send multiple requests simultaneously. However, each student can only accept one request per counsellor. If a student is already connected to you, trying to send another request will show an error.

What Happens When You Send a Request

When you send a connection request:

  • The student receives a notification in Compass that you've requested access
  • They can review a summary of what you'll be able to see (readiness score, milestone progress, etc.)
  • They have the choice to accept or decline
  • You receive real-time notification of their response
  • If accepted, you immediately gain access to their dashboard

Request Statuses

Each request has one of four statuses:

Pending

The student has been invited but has not yet responded. The request is active for 30 days. After 30 days, the request expires automatically.

Accepted

The student has accepted your request. You now have access to their dashboard. This status remains active until the student disconnects or you manually remove the connection.

Declined

The student has declined your request. You cannot see their data. You can send another request, but the student must explicitly accept it.

Expired

The request was not responded to within 30 days and has automatically expired. You can resend the request at any time.

Validation Rules

Before a connection request is sent, the system validates several conditions:

  • Student must have a Compass account registered with that email address
  • No duplicate requests: You cannot send multiple pending requests to the same student
  • Valid email format (proper @ and domain structure)
  • Your account must be in good standing (not suspended or flagged)

Error Messages Explained

The email address you entered is not registered with a Compass account. Verify the email is correct and ask the student to confirm it matches their Compass registration.

You've already sent a request to this student and are waiting for their response. If the request has expired (30+ days), you can resend it. Otherwise, wait for the student to respond or decline the pending request first.

You have an active connection with this student. You can see their dashboard. If you wish to disconnect, manage the connection from "My Students" or wait for the student to disconnect.

The email address you entered doesn't follow the standard format (name@domain.com). Check for typos or extra spaces.

Resending Declined Requests

If a student has declined your request, you can send another request at any time. However:

  • There is no cool-down period—you can resend immediately
  • It's advisable to reach out personally to discuss why they declined and reassure them about privacy
  • The student will see another notification in Compass
  • Multiple declined requests may signal that the student is not comfortable sharing; respect their decision
📋 Student Privacy & Choice

If a student repeatedly declines, do not pressure them to accept. Their decision to decline is valid and reflects their comfort level with sharing. Focus on building trust through direct conversations about how you can support their applications.

What You Can & Cannot See

Understanding access boundaries protects student privacy and ensures FERPA, GDPR, and other compliance requirements are met.

What You CAN See

✓ Readiness Score (Overall & Breakdown)

Complete readiness scores across all five dimensions: Language, Academic, Activities, Essays, Milestones. This is central to your role as a counsellor.

✓ Test Scores & Results

SAT, ACT, TOEFL, IELTS, CAE, IB, A-level, AP, and other standardized test scores (if the student has logged them in Compass).

✓ Extracurricular Activities

List of activities, roles, hours committed, and years of involvement. You see the breadth and depth of a student's engagement.

✓ School List & Application Status

Schools where the student is applying, tier classifications (Reach, Match, Safe), and application status (Not Started, In Progress, Submitted, Decision).

✓ Milestone Progress & Overdue Milestones

All upcoming deadlines, completion status, and flagged overdue milestones. This is essential for accountability.

✓ Profile Status & Last Check-In Date

Overall status (Strong Profile, Good Progress, Early Stage), and the date/time of the student's most recent Compass login.

✓ Grade Level & Target Countries

Basic demographic info and geographic preferences for college search.

What You CANNOT See

✗ Essay Content

You cannot view the text of any college essays or personal statements. You see only completion status ("Draft," "In Progress," "Submitted") but not the actual content. This protects student intellectual property and privacy.

✗ Journal Entries & Reflections

Compass allows students to journal privately about their application journey. These entries are never shared with counsellors, even if connected.

✗ Monthly Check-In Responses

Students fill out monthly self-reflection forms about their progress and emotions. These are confidential and not visible to you.

✗ Passwords & Authentication Data

You never see any passwords, authentication tokens, or security credentials.

✗ Parent / Guardian Information

If a parent has an account linked to a student, you cannot see parent records or communications.

✗ Previous Years' Data (if applicable)

You see only the student's current Compass record. Historical data from prior years is not accessible.

Why This Matters

📋 Student Privacy & Trust

The boundaries between what you can and cannot see are intentional. They balance your legitimate need to monitor application progress with the student's right to privacy. Journal entries and monthly reflections are therapeutic spaces; essays are intellectual property. Respecting these boundaries builds trust and models healthy data practices.

📋 Safeguarding Principle

Access limitations protect vulnerable students. Some students may disclose sensitive information (mental health concerns, family stress) in essays or journal entries. By not sharing these, we ensure the counsellor role remains focused on application readiness, not therapy.

⚠️ Do Not Request Restricted Data

As a counsellor, you may be tempted to ask students for essay drafts or journal reflections directly. While you can request these voluntarily through direct conversation, you must not pressure students or threaten to revoke support if they decline. Respect their boundaries.

Accessing Information Outside Compass Counsellor

If you need information beyond what Compass Counsellor displays (e.g., a student's essay draft), you have two options:

  1. Ask the student directly in a separate conversation (in-person, email, etc.). They can choose to share or not.
  2. Have the student share it through Compass — They can upload documents or notes to Compass, which may sync to the shared dashboard.

Never demand access to private data. The student's consent and comfort are paramount.

Managing Connections

Understand how connections work, how students can disconnect, and how to manage multiple student relationships.

Student-Initiated Disconnect

Students have the right to disconnect from you at any time, for any reason. When they disconnect:

  • Your access is revoked immediately. You can no longer see their dashboard.
  • The connection is permanent until the student re-invites you.
  • You receive a notification that the student has disconnected.
  • The student appears as "Disconnected" in your Requests tab (you can optionally send a new request).
📋 Why Students Disconnect

Students may disconnect for various reasons: changed mind about sharing, privacy concerns, or they're no longer working with you. Always respect their decision. If you believe a disconnection was accidental, reach out personally (not through Compass).

What Happens When a Student Disconnects

From your perspective:

  • The student's row in "My Students" changes to "Disconnected" status.
  • You no longer see real-time updates to their dashboard.
  • You can view a snapshot of their final recorded data (for 30 days), then it's archived.
  • You cannot contact them through Compass Counsellor.
  • You can send a new connection request if you wish to reconnect later.
⚠️ No Re-access Without Re-invite

Once a student disconnects, you cannot access their data again unless they explicitly re-invite you. You cannot force or re-establish the connection unilaterally.

Counsellor-Initiated Disconnect (Optional Feature)

In some configurations, you may have the ability to initiate a disconnect on your own (e.g., if a student graduates or transfers). If this feature is enabled:

  • Open the student's detail panel.
  • Click the three-dot menu → "Disconnect."
  • Confirm the action (this is permanent).
  • The student receives a notification that you have disconnected.
📋 When to Disconnect

Use this feature thoughtfully. Common scenarios: student graduates, transfers to another school, or you are no longer their assigned counsellor. Always notify the student directly (outside Compass) before disconnecting if possible.

Managing Multiple Students

There is no limit to the number of students you can connect with. You can:

  • Add students incrementally as they accept requests
  • Monitor entire cohorts on a single dashboard
  • Sort and filter by grade, readiness score, or school list
  • Track summary metrics (average readiness, at-risk count) across all students

Use the search and sort features to keep your cohort organized as your roster grows.

Connection Lifecycle

Here's a visual summary of a connection's lifecycle:

  1. You send a request → Status: Pending
  2. Student accepts → Status: Connected (Active)
  3. You monitor their progress → Real-time dashboard updates
  4. Student disconnects (or request expires) → Status: Disconnected / Expired
  5. Optional: Send a new request → Status: Pending (returns to Step 1)
💡 Pro Tip: Document the Date

Keep a record of when you connect with each student (external to Compass Counsellor). This helps you track cohort changes and remember context if a student later reconnects.

Data Obligations & Compliance

As a counsellor using Compass Counsellor, you are a data processor with specific legal and ethical responsibilities. This section details all 14 obligations.

⚠️ Critical: You Accepted These on Sign-Up

By creating your account, you confirmed you understand and accept these obligations. Failure to comply may result in account suspension, legal liability, or harm to students. Review this section carefully and consult your school's legal or IT department if you have questions.

1. Data Controller Role

You are a data controller (or authorized processor) under data protection laws:

  • You determine what student data to access and how to use it.
  • You are accountable for compliance with regulations in your jurisdiction.
  • Your school or organisation may share responsibility (consult your Data Protection Officer).

2. FERPA (Family Educational Rights & Privacy Act)

If you operate in the United States (or serve US students):

Legitimate Educational Interest

You may access student records only if you have a legitimate educational interest—defined as a need to perform your duties as a counsellor (e.g., monitoring college application progress). General curiosity is not sufficient.

Re-Disclosure Prohibition

You cannot disclose student data to third parties without the student's written consent. This includes:

  • Other teachers or staff (unless they also have a legitimate educational interest)
  • Parents or guardians (unless authorized by the student or school policy)
  • Colleges or universities (unless the student explicitly consents)
  • The media, social networks, or public communications
⚠️ FERPA Violations Are Serious

Breaching FERPA can result in loss of federal funding, legal action, and personal liability. When in doubt, do not share student data.

3. COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act)

If you serve students under 13 years old (rare for college prep, but possible):

  • School exception applies: Schools can collect data on children under 13 without parental consent for educational purposes.
  • You must be a school agent: Only school employees or contractors (not private advisors) may collect data this way.
  • Parent notification recommended: Even though not legally required, notifying parents builds trust and transparency.

4. GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

If you serve students in the EU or UK:

Lawful Basis: Consent

GDPR requires a lawful basis for processing personal data. For Compass Counsellor, the basis is explicit student consent:

  • The student must actively invite you and accept your connection.
  • Consent must be informed (student understands what data they're sharing).
  • Consent is freely given (no coercion or pressure).

Data Subject Rights

Students have rights under GDPR:

  • Right to access: Student can request all data you hold.
  • Right to rectification: Student can correct inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure: Student can request deletion (subject to retention obligations).
  • Right to withdraw consent: Disconnect at any time (equivalent to erasure).
📋 GDPR Compliance Checklist

✓ Student has given explicit consent (accepted connection request)
✓ You process data only for college application support
✓ You do not share data with third parties without consent
✓ You keep data secure (strong password, no sharing)
✓ You respect withdrawal of consent (disconnect)

5. PDPA (Singapore Personal Data Protection Act)

If you serve students in Singapore:

  • Consent-based: Like GDPR, PDPA requires consent. Student's acceptance of your connection request satisfies this.
  • Purpose limitation: Use data only for college counselling; do not repurpose it.
  • Security obligations: Maintain strong access controls and encryption.
  • Notification requirement: If a breach occurs, notify the Personal Data Protection Commission within 30 days.

6. Data Access Scope

You commit to accessing only the data displayed in Compass Counsellor:

  • Do not attempt to access Compass student accounts. You do not have login credentials and should never try to obtain them.
  • Do not access backend systems or APIs. Use only the Compass Counsellor interface provided.
  • Honour access restrictions. Some data (essays, journal entries, check-ins) is intentionally hidden. Do not circumvent these.

7. Data Retention & Deletion

You must manage data retention responsibly:

  • Retain for counselling purposes only: Keep data as long as you are actively supporting a student's college process.
  • Delete on disconnect: When a student disconnects or you disconnect from them, do not retain a separate copy of their data (unless required by law for record-keeping).
  • Compliance with school policy: Follow your school's record retention policy (typically 3–7 years for guidance records).
  • No personal copies: Do not download or export student data to personal files (email, cloud storage, USB drives). Use Compass Counsellor as the source of truth.
⚠️ Prohibition on Exporting Data

Compass Counsellor does not support bulk export of student data. This is intentional. If you need to share information about a student (e.g., with parents, the school principal), do so verbally or in writing based on your memory—do not copy/paste from Compass.

8. AI Processing Disclosure

Compass Counsellor may use AI to calculate readiness scores and provide insights:

  • Automated scoring: Readiness scores are calculated by algorithms, not humans. They are advisory, not definitive.
  • No profiling: AI is not used to make decisions about student potential or recommend actions. Those decisions are yours.
  • Transparency: Students are informed in Compass that scores are AI-calculated.
  • Human review obligation: You must review scores in context of the student's full profile before acting on them.
📋 Interpreting Scores Responsibly

A student with a score of 42 ("Early Stage") is not "low-achieving." The score reflects readiness progress, not ability. Students often start low and improve rapidly. Use scores as a conversation starter, not a label.

9. Security Obligations

You are responsible for securing your account and the data you access:

Strong Passwords

  • Minimum 8 characters
  • Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols
  • Unique (do not reuse passwords from other sites)
  • Change every 6 months or if compromised
  • Never share your password with colleagues, even trusted ones

Shared Device Security

  • Always log out of Compass Counsellor when you step away from a shared computer (school lab, shared office PC).
  • Set a timer reminder if you tend to forget.
  • Do not access Compass Counsellor from public WiFi without a VPN.

Physical Security

  • Lock your computer when you leave your desk.
  • Do not leave written notes with student scores or readiness summaries visible on your desk.
  • Close the Compass Counsellor window if colleagues or students walk by.

10. Australian Privacy Act (APPs)

If you serve students in Australia:

  • Collection & use: Collect student data only for college counselling and disclose the purpose upfront (usually satisfied by the student's acceptance).
  • Data security: Take reasonable steps to protect data from misuse and loss.
  • Access & correction: Students may request to see their data and correct errors.
  • Overseas disclosure: If data is transferred overseas (e.g., Compass servers), inform the student and ensure equivalent protections.

11. PIPEDA (Canada Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)

If you serve students in Canada:

  • Consent: Student's acceptance of your connection request constitutes informed consent.
  • Use limitation: Use data only for college counselling; do not repurpose for marketing, research, or other aims.
  • Accuracy: Keep records accurate and up-to-date (reliance on Compass is acceptable).
  • Openness: Be transparent about your data practices. Answer student questions about what you collect and how you use it.

12. UAE PDPL (Personal Data Protection Law)

If you serve students in the United Arab Emirates:

  • Consent: Student's acceptance of your connection is valid consent.
  • Special categories: If you access sensitive data (health, religion, etc.), note stricter protections apply. Stick to college application data only.
  • Data localization: Some data may be required to stay within the UAE. Consult your school's IT department.
  • Breach notification: Report breaches to the UAEDPB (UAE Data Protection Bureau) and affected students within 72 hours.

13. Child Safety & Safeguarding

You have a duty of care to protect student welfare:

KCSIE (UK) & Equivalent Standards

In the UK, you must follow Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE). In other countries, follow equivalent safeguarding standards (e.g., UNCRC, state-level mandates).

UNCRC Article 3 (Child's Best Interest)

All actions affecting children must prioritize the child's best interest. When interpreting student data (low readiness score, overdue milestones), consider the child's circumstances and offer support, not criticism.

Mandatory Reporting

If a student discloses abuse, neglect, or serious harm (in Compass messages, essay content you're aware of, or a conversation with you), you have a legal duty to report:

  • Report to your school's safeguarding team or principal immediately.
  • Do not conduct your own investigation.
  • Do not tell the student you're reporting (consult your school's safeguarding policy; some require this, others advise against it).
  • Keep detailed notes of what was disclosed.

Safeguarding Protocol

  • Never dismiss concerns. If a student says, "I'm really struggling," take it seriously.
  • Refer to specialists. If a student has mental health concerns, refer to a school counsellor or psychologist, not just your college coaching.
  • Maintain confidentiality. Do not gossip about student disclosures to colleagues; share only with those with a need to know.
  • Your role has limits. You are a college counsellor, not a therapist. Recognize when a student needs professional mental health support.
⚠️ Safeguarding is Non-Negotiable

If you ever believe a student is at risk of harm, escalate immediately. Overreporting is always safer than underreporting. Your school has safeguarding procedures; use them.

14. EU AI Act (High-Risk Classification)

If you serve EU students, note that Compass Counsellor uses AI for readiness scoring:

  • High-risk disclosure: You are informed that AI is used. This is not a hidden tool.
  • Advisory only: Scores are advisory, not determinative. Human judgment (yours) is essential.
  • Explanation obligation: If a student questions a score, you can explain the factors that contributed (language, academics, activities, essays, milestones), but the exact algorithm is proprietary.
  • Human reviewer obligation: You must personally review scores and data before making recommendations to students.
  • No automated decisions: Never make decisions about a student (advise to retake SAT, discourage them from applying to a school) based on a score alone. Always discuss in context.

15. Content Moderation & Academic Integrity

As you monitor students, you may encounter content that raises concerns:

Plagiarism Suspicion

If you suspect a student has plagiarized an essay or borrowed heavily from another source, do not accuse directly in Compass. Instead:

  • Schedule a private conversation outside Compass.
  • Ask the student to explain their essay (how they developed the idea, their research process).
  • If plagiarism is confirmed, report to your school's academic integrity committee.
  • Use Compass Counsellor tools (if available) to flag the student as at-risk academically, but do not make public accusations.

Harmful Content

If a student's essays, activities, or profile contain harmful, illegal, or hateful content, report it:

  • Do not publicly shame the student in Compass.
  • Report to your school's administration or compliance team.
  • Contact SAIL Education compliance at compliance@sailedu.co if the issue involves the Compass platform itself.

16. Breach Notification (72-Hour Rule)

If you suspect a data breach (unauthorized access, lost device, hacked account), you must act immediately:

  1. Stop using the account if hacked (change password immediately).
  2. Notify your school's IT security team or Data Protection Officer.
  3. Contact SAIL Education compliance at compliance@sailedu.co with details of the breach.
  4. Provide affected student names and what data was accessed.
  5. Affected students and authorities must be notified within 72 hours (per GDPR and similar laws).
⚠️ Breach Examples

• Your Compass Counsellor password is compromised
• You accidentally email a student's readiness score to the wrong recipient
• A colleague accesses your Compass Counsellor account without permission
• You lose a device with Compass Counsellor notes saved locally
• A hacker gains access to your email (and could access Compass password reset links)

📋 Compliance Contact

Email: compliance@sailedu.co
Subject line: "[URGENT] Data Breach Report – [Your School Name]"
Include: names of affected students, type of data accessed, how it happened, steps taken to mitigate.

Target Tracking

What is Target Tracking?

Set individual readiness score targets for each student, then track their progress toward those targets. This gives you a personalized gap metric for every student—essential for tailored coaching strategies.

Setting a Target

  1. Open Student Detail Panel: Click on any student's row in the dashboard
  2. Find "Target Score" field: In the readiness score section (right side of panel)
  3. Enter target (0–100): e.g., "75" for a Reach school application; "85" for top-tier targets
  4. Click Save: Target is saved to your session (you can update anytime)

Understanding Your Gap

Gap = Target − Current Readiness Score

Example: Target 80, Current Score 62 → Gap: +18 points

The dashboard sorts students by gap size (largest gaps first), helping you prioritize support for students furthest from their goals.

Strategies by Gap Size

Gap Size Student Status Coaching Focus
0–5 points On track or exceeding target Refine weakest area; prepare finalization
6–15 points Progressing well Accelerate two strongest areas; shore up one weak area
16–30 points Needs attention Log interventions; identify blockers
30+ points Significant gap Urgent intervention; consider lowering target or extending timeline

Tips

💡 Setting realistic targets:
  • For Reach schools (top 10% selectivity): 80–95
  • For Match schools (realistic fit): 70–80
  • For Safe schools (likely acceptance): 60–70

Safeguarding Log

What is the Safeguarding Log?

A confidential per-student log for documenting concerns about a student's wellbeing, mental health, or safety. This is required in many jurisdictions (e.g., UK KCSIE, US Title IX) and helps you respond appropriately to safeguarding issues.

When to Log an Entry

Log when you notice:

  • Signs of anxiety, depression, or stress beyond typical application stress
  • Self-harm language or suicidal ideation (immediate: contact emergency services)
  • Pressure from parents (coercive behavior, financial threats)
  • Bullying or peer pressure affecting school choices
  • Substance abuse concerns
  • Abuse or neglect indicators
  • Unexpected behavioral changes
  • Attendance patterns that suggest a problem

Creating a Log Entry

  1. Open Student Panel
  2. Scroll to "Safeguarding Log"
  3. Click "+ Add Entry"
  4. Fill in:
    • Date & time: When you observed the concern
    • Severity: Low, Medium, High, Critical
    • Category: Mental health, Abuse, Peer, Family, Other
    • Observation: What you saw/heard (facts, not assumptions). E.g., "Student mentioned 'can't stop thinking about suicide' in check-in response"
    • Your response: What action you took (called parent, referred to counselor, contacted safeguarding lead)
  5. Save: Entry is logged with timestamp

Privacy & Access

Confidentiality: Safeguarding logs are:

  • Encrypted and accessible only to you and designated safeguarding leads
  • NOT visible to the student (even if they access their own profile)
  • Retained indefinitely (do not delete)
  • Shared with authorities only when legally required (abuse disclosure, imminent danger)

Escalation Protocol

For "Critical" severity entries:

  1. Contact your school's designated safeguarding lead IMMEDIATELY (not within 24 hours)
  2. If imminent danger (active self-harm), call emergency services
  3. Document your actions in the log entry
  4. Do NOT promise confidentiality to the student if their safety is at risk
⚠️ Legal obligation: Many jurisdictions require you to report suspected abuse to child protective services, even if the student asks you not to. Consult your school's safeguarding policy and legal counsel.

Training & Resources

  • Your school should provide annual safeguarding training
  • Contact your safeguarding lead if you're unsure whether to log an entry
  • Resources: Childhelp National Hotline (US: 1-800-4-A-CHILD), NSPCC (UK), or your local equivalent
