Welcome to SAIL Helm

SAIL Helm is your comprehensive administrative interface for SAIL OS (Schoolwide AI Learning Operating System), an integrated educational platform designed for K-12 institutions. SAIL OS v6 represents the latest evolution in AI-powered learning management, combining traditional course management with intelligent tutoring, real-time analytics, and comprehensive safeguarding features.

What is SAIL OS?

SAIL OS is a complete learning ecosystem that empowers educators to create engaging courses, monitor student progress in real-time, leverage AI-powered content creation and tutoring, and make data-driven decisions about student learning and wellbeing. It brings together course content, student data, assessment tools, AI learning assistants, and comprehensive analytics under one intuitive platform.

AI-Powered Learning - SAIL OS integrates advanced AI capabilities throughout the platform, from automated content generation to real-time learning support, all designed with safety guardrails and educational best practices.

First-Time Setup: Provisioning Wizard

Super Admin

The Provisioning Wizard is a comprehensive 8-step setup process that guides Super Administrators through the initial configuration of SAIL OS. This wizard ensures your school is properly configured with all necessary data, compliance settings, and user access controls before launch.

Overview of the Provisioning Process

The Provisioning Wizard takes approximately 30-60 minutes to complete, depending on your school's size and data complexity. You can save your progress and return to complete it later. All steps must be completed before launching SAIL OS in production.

Pro Tip: Gather all necessary information before starting the wizard. Have CSVs ready for staff and student imports, and ensure you have your school's DPA information available.

Step-by-Step Provisioning Walkthrough

Step 1: School Identity

Configure your school's basic information.

  1. Click "Start Provisioning Wizard" from the Administration dashboard
  2. Enter your school name (e.g., "Lincoln High School")
  3. Select your region/country from the dropdown
  4. Enter your Data Protection Authority (DPA) email address for compliance notifications
  5. Upload your school logo (optional, recommended for branded emails)
  6. Click "Next" to proceed to Step 2
Important: The DPA email address should be a monitored account, as SAIL OS will send compliance-related notifications to this address.

Step 2: Staff Import

Import your teaching and administrative staff into SAIL OS.

  1. Prepare a CSV file with staff information using the following columns:
    • first_name (required)
    • last_name (required)
    • email (required, must be unique)
    • role (required: Super Admin, Admin, Dept Head, or Teacher)
    • department (optional: Math, Science, English, etc.)
    • phone (optional)
    • employee_id (optional)
  2. Click "Upload CSV" and select your staff file
  3. Review the preview to ensure data is correct
  4. Correct any errors shown in the validation report
  5. Click "Import Staff" to proceed

Step 3: Student Import

Import your student population with age-gating compliance.

  1. Prepare a CSV file with student information using these columns:
    • first_name (required)
    • last_name (required)
    • email (required, must be unique)
    • student_id (required)
    • grade (required: K-12)
    • date_of_birth (required: YYYY-MM-DD for age-gating)
    • parent_email (recommended for students under 13)
    • parent_phone (optional)
  2. Click "Upload CSV" and select your student file
  3. SAIL OS automatically flags students under 13 and verifies parent consent
  4. Review the age-gating report
  5. Click "Import Students" to proceed
Age-Gating Compliance: SAIL OS automatically applies COPPA compliance for students under 13, including parental consent verification and restricted AI feature access.

Step 4: Course Creation

Set up your initial course structure.

  1. Click "Create Courses" or "Import from Template"
  2. For manual creation, enter course details:
    • Course Name (e.g., "Biology 101")
    • Course Code (e.g., "BIO101")
    • Color (for visual identification in dashboards)
    • Framework (CEFR, IELTS, International, or Custom)
    • Description and learning outcomes
  3. Assign lead teacher(s) for each course
  4. Click "Save Course" and continue adding courses
  5. Click "Next" when finished

Step 5: Enrollment

Enroll students in courses.

  1. Select a course from your created courses
  2. Use the student picker to select students or import from CSV
  3. Enrollment CSV should contain: student_email, course_code
  4. Verify enrollment counts for accuracy
  5. Repeat for each course
  6. Click "Next" when all enrollments are complete

Step 6: Historical Data Import

Optionally import grades and attendance from your previous system.

  1. Click "Skip" if you're starting fresh, or "Import" to add legacy data
  2. If importing, upload CSVs for:
    • Grade history (student_email, course_code, grade, date)
    • Attendance records (student_email, date, status)
    • Prior assignments (optional)
  3. Review validation report for any errors
  4. Correct and re-upload if needed
  5. Click "Next" to proceed

Step 7: Compliance Review

Verify your school's compliance configuration.

  1. Review the compliance checklist:
    • GDPR compliance (if applicable)
    • FERPA safeguards confirmed
    • COPPA age-gating verified
    • Data retention policy set
    • Audit logging enabled
  2. Enable 2FA for all admin accounts
  3. Configure backup and disaster recovery settings
  4. Sign the compliance acknowledgment
  5. Click "Next" to proceed to launch

Step 8: Launch SAIL

Complete final verification and launch your SAIL OS instance.

  1. Review the "Ready to Launch" checklist
  2. Verify all data imports completed successfully
  3. Confirm administrative access for key staff
  4. Review the deployment timeline and any scheduled maintenance windows
  5. Click "Launch SAIL OS" to activate the platform
  6. You will receive a confirmation email when launch is complete
Post-Launch: After launch, send welcome emails to all staff and students with login instructions and links to this manual.

Troubleshooting Common Import Issues

Duplicate email addresses: Email addresses must be unique. Check for typos or students/staff using shared accounts.

Invalid date formats: Dates must be in YYYY-MM-DD format. Ensure your CSV export settings match this format.

Missing required fields: All required columns must be present in your CSV, even if some rows have empty values.

File encoding issues: Save CSVs as UTF-8 encoded files to avoid character encoding errors.

Support Resources: If you encounter issues during provisioning, contact SAIL Education support or consult the API documentation for advanced import options.

Course Management is the foundation of SAIL OS, allowing educators to create, organize, and manage all aspects of course delivery. From curriculum design to content versioning, Course Management provides comprehensive tools for educational planning and delivery.

What is a Course?

In SAIL OS, a course is a structured learning experience with defined learning outcomes, content materials, assignments, assessments, and a roster of enrolled students. Courses can be designed for a single class or can serve multiple sections with shared curriculum.

Creating a New Course

  1. Navigate to "Course Management" from the main menu
  2. Click "Create New Course" button
  3. Enter the course name (e.g., "AP Biology 2025-2026")
  4. Enter the course code (e.g., "BIO-AP-01")
  5. Select a color for visual identification across the platform
  6. Select a curriculum framework:
    • CEFR (Common European Framework) - Language courses
    • IELTS - English language proficiency
    • International Baccalaureate (IB) - Advanced curriculum
    • Custom - Define your own learning outcomes
  7. Write course description and learning outcomes
  8. Assign lead teacher(s)
  9. Click "Create Course"
Framework Selection: The framework you choose determines the available rubrics, grading scales, and learning outcome templates. This cannot be changed after course creation.

Course Structure and Organization

Once created, organize your course content using the following hierarchical structure:

Level Purpose Example
Course Overall class or subject Biology 101
Unit Major topic grouping (4-8 weeks) Cell Structure and Function
Module Lesson-level content (1-2 weeks) Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic Cells
Lesson Single lesson or activity (1-3 days) Cell Membrane Properties
Content Items Individual resources and assignments Video, worksheet, quiz

Adding Course Materials

  1. Click into your course and select "Course Materials"
  2. Click "Create Unit" to start organizing content
  3. Enter unit name, description, and duration
  4. For each unit, create modules by clicking "Add Module"
  5. Within modules, add lessons and content items
  6. Use the drag-and-drop interface to reorder content
  7. Click "Publish Unit" when ready to make it visible to students

Creating and Using Rubrics

Rubrics provide detailed assessment criteria for assignments and help ensure consistent grading.

  1. Navigate to your course and click "Rubrics"
  2. Click "Create New Rubric"
  3. Enter rubric name and description
  4. Add evaluation criteria (e.g., "Clarity of Thesis")
  5. For each criterion, define performance levels:
    • Excellent (e.g., "4 points")
    • Proficient (e.g., "3 points")
    • Developing (e.g., "2 points")
    • Beginning (e.g., "1 point")
  6. Write descriptors for each level
  7. Click "Save Rubric"
  8. Attach the rubric to assignments when creating them

  2. Select the grade level(s)
  3. All students in that grade will be automatically enrolled
  4. You can remove individual students if needed

Course Versioning

SAIL OS automatically maintains version history for your course, allowing you to revert changes or manage multiple course instances.

  1. Click into your course and select "Version History"

  3. Click a version to view its contents
  4. Click "Restore" to revert to that version
  5. Create named versions by clicking "Save as Version"
Best Practice: Save versioned copies of your course at the end of each school year, making it easy to import and customize for the next year.

Assignments & Grading is the core instructional interface in SAIL OS, enabling teachers to create assessments, track submissions, grade work, and provide feedback. The system features AI-powered integrity checking and comprehensive gradebook management.

Creating Assignments

  1. Navigate to your course and click "Assignments"
  2. Click "Create New Assignment"
  3. Enter assignment title and description
  4. Choose assignment type:
    • Written Submission (essays, responses)
    • Multiple Choice / Quiz

    • Presentation / Recording
    • Peer Review
  5. Set point value and grading scale
  6. Attach a rubric (optional but recommended)
  7. Set due date and submission deadline
  8. Enable late submission options if desired
  9. Click "Create Assignment"

Differentiated Assignments

SAIL OS supports differentiated learning with tiered assignment versions:

Creating Three-Tier Assignments

  1. When creating an assignment, toggle "Create Differentiated Versions"
  2. Create three versions:
    • Foundation: Simpler task, fewer requirements, lower point value
    • Standard: Grade-level expectations
    • Extension: Advanced challenge, higher rigor
  3. Assign students to appropriate tiers based on their level
  4. All versions appear on the same assignment in the gradebook
  5. View completion by tier in assignment analytics
Best Practice: Use differentiated assignments to ensure all learners are appropriately challenged while maintaining classroom cohesion.

AI Integrity Checking

SAIL OS includes sophisticated AI integrity checking to detect potential academic integrity concerns in written submissions. The system uses a 5-signal approach:

Signal What It Measures Interpretation
Lexical Predictability How predictable word choices are AI writing uses more predictable vocabulary patterns
Stylometric Shift Changes in writing style within a submission Abrupt shifts suggest copied or pasted content
Structural Uniformity Paragraph and sentence structure consistency Overly uniform structure suggests non-human origin
Source Corroboration Comparison with publicly available sources Significant similarity to online sources
AI Likelihood Score Overall probability of AI generation Composite score (0-100%) combining all signals
Using Integrity Checks Responsibly: SAIL OS integrity checking is a screening tool, not a definitive proof. Always investigate flagged submissions and use professional judgment. False positives occur with technical writing, translated content, and ESL students.

Grading Submissions

  1. Click "Assignments" and select the assignment to grade
  2. Click "View Submissions" to see all student work
  3. Click a student's submission to open the grading interface

Peer Review Process

Enable peer review for assignments to promote collaborative learning:

  1. When creating an assignment, toggle "Enable Peer Review"
  2. Set the number of peers each student reviews (typically 2-3)
  3. Create a peer review rubric with evaluation criteria
  4. Set review deadline (typically 3-5 days after submission deadline)
  5. SAIL OS randomly assigns reviewers
  6. Students complete peer reviews using the rubric
  7. You receive peer review summary to inform your own grading
Important: Peer reviews do not automatically affect student grades. Use them as formative feedback and reference for your own assessment.

Bulk Grading Tools

  3. Save and upload back to SAIL OS
  4. Review import preview before confirming

Quick Grade Entry

  1. Click "Grade Assignment"
  2. View all submissions in a table
  3. Click cells to enter grades directly
  4. Use Tab key to move between students
  5. Click "Save All Grades" when finished
Grading Tip: Grade assignments in multiple sessions if working with large classes. Save your progress frequently.

Tier 2: Warn Content is published but flagged; teacher is notified Moderate: bullying, inappropriate language, misinformation

At-Risk Intervention Workflow

Comprehensive process for supporting students showing multiple risk indicators:

  1. Risk Identification: System flags students with:
    • Declining grades (2+ grade drops)
    • Chronic absenteeism (6+ days)
    • Low mood check-ins (consistent 1-2 ratings)

  2. Initial Assessment: Click on flagged student to review all risk signals
  3. Intervention Planning: Click "Create Intervention Plan" and document:
    • Risk factors identified
    • Specific support strategies
    • Responsible adults

  4. Family Communication: Generate letter to parents notifying them of supports offered
  5. Monitoring: Track intervention progress with regular check-ins
  6. Resolution: Mark intervention as resolved or escalate if needed

Safeguarding Protocol

SAIL OS includes a three-tier safeguarding process for serious concerns:

Tier 1: Low Concern

Triggers: Isolated mood report of 1-2, first absence pattern detected

Actions: Teacher conversation, informal check-in, monitoring

Tier 2: Medium Concern

Triggers: Multiple risk signals, persistent mood issues, behavioral changes

Actions: School counselor involvement, parent meeting, formal support plan

Tier 3: High Concern

Triggers: Immediate safety concerns, severe distress, self-harm indicators

Actions:

  • School admin notified immediately
  • School counselor engaged
  • Parents contacted
  • Emergency protocols activated if needed
  • Documentation in secure audit trail

Important: If a student is in immediate danger, call emergency services (911) immediately. SAIL OS safeguarding is a support tool, not a replacement for professional crisis intervention.

Compliance

SAIL OS is designed to meet major educational and data privacy regulations. This section outlines our compliance commitments.

FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)

Applies to: All US K-12 schools

SAIL OS ensures FERPA compliance through:

  • Role-based access controls restricting student data access
  • Parents can access only their child's records
  • Students 18+ can control parental access
  • Complete audit trails for all data access
  • Secure data destruction at course/year end
  • Annual FERPA compliance training recommended

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

Applies to: Schools in EU or serving EU students

SAIL OS GDPR features:

  • Data Processing Agreement (DPA) available for all schools
  • Legal basis established for each data processing activity
  • User consent management for optional data uses
  • Right to access, rectification, erasure, and portability
  • Data retention policies configurable by school
  • Breach notification within 72 hours
  • Automated impact assessments for high-risk processing

COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act)

Applies to: US schools serving students under 13

SAIL OS COPPA compliance:

  • Automatic age-gating for students under 13
  • Parental consent verification required
  • Restricted data collection for young students
  • No third-party advertising or tracking
  • Limited AI features for under-13 students
  • Parental access to all collected information

KCSIE (Keeping Children Safe in Education)

Applies to: UK schools

SAIL OS KCSIE features:

  • Safeguarding protocols with three-tier intervention system
  • Staff training tracking and compliance
  • Incident logging and reporting capabilities
  • Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) access to support tools
  • Integration with external safeguarding agencies
  • Wellbeing monitoring and at-risk identification

Online Safety Act

Applies to: UK online platforms serving minors

SAIL OS Online Safety features:

  • Age-appropriate content safeguards
  • AI moderation of user-generated content
  • Transparent reporting of moderation decisions
  • User reporting mechanism for harmful content
  • Data minimization for under-18 users
  • Parental controls and monitoring tools

EU AI Act

Applies to: EU schools using AI features

SAIL OS AI compliance:

  • Risk assessment for all AI-powered features
  • Transparency documentation for AI systems
  • Human oversight of critical AI decisions (grading, safeguarding)
  • Bias testing and fairness audits
  • No high-risk AI used for student assessment
  • Regular third-party AI audits

UNESCO AI and Education Guidance

SAIL OS aligns with UNESCO's principles:

  • Promotes inclusive and equitable education
  • Protects cultural and linguistic diversity
  • Maintains human agency and responsibility
  • Transparent about AI limitations
  • Regular stakeholder engagement

UNCRC (UN Convention on the Rights of the Child)

SAIL OS respects child rights:

  • Right to education - platform supports inclusive learning
