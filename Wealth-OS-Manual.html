What is Wealth-OS?

Wealth-OS is a personal wealth management platform that combines three key capabilities:

  • Portfolio Tracking: Real-time visibility into your holdings, performance, and allocation across three strategic layers.
  • AI Behavioral Coach: An intelligent advisor that guides you through investment decisions, enforces portfolio rules, and asks "why" before you trade.
  • Retirement Projections: Multi-scenario growth calculator showing how different contribution and market assumptions impact your long-term wealth.

The 3-Layer Philosophy

Wealth-OS organizes your portfolio into three distinct layers, each serving a different purpose in your overall strategy:

Foundation (40%)

  • Capital preservation
  • High liquidity
  • Minimal volatility
  • Emergency buffer

Growth (40%)

  • Long-term compounding
  • Diversified equities
  • Bond allocation
  • Moderate risk

Asymmetric (20%)

  • High-risk, high-upside
  • Crypto optionality
  • Limited downside
  • Conviction plays

This structure ensures your core wealth is protected while maintaining exposure to growth and opportunity. The system is designed for personal use and long-term wealth building.

The 3-Layer System Explained

Deep dive into Wealth-OS's foundational architecture: why layers work, how they protect your wealth, and the rules that govern each.

Why Layers Matter

Traditional portfolios often blend everything together, leading to unclear risk management and poor decision-making. The 3-Layer System separates your wealth by purpose and risk level:

Foundation Layer (40% target, Blue #1560A8)

Your financial bedrock. This is capital preservation and liquidity. Think of it as your emergency fund on steroids.

Growth Layer (40% target, Teal #0A7A58)

Your long-term wealth engine. Diversified equities, bonds, and index funds that compound over decades.

Asymmetric Layer (20% target, Gold #B07010)

Your optionality bucket. High-risk, high-upside plays where you can win 10x but lose your allocation.

Foundation Layer (40%)

Purpose

Capital preservation, liquidity, and emergency buffer. This is not growth capital; it's sleeping capital ready for crisis or opportunity.

Target Allocation

40% of your total portfolio value.

Suitable Holdings

  • Cash (USD, GBP, EUR)
  • Money market funds (VMFXX, SPAXX)
  • Short-term Treasury bonds (SHV, IEF)
  • High-yield savings equivalents
  • Ultra-safe index funds (bonds only)

Rules

  • Minimum volatility: Holdings should have <5% expected annual volatility
  • High liquidity: Must be convertible to cash within 1 business day
  • Capital preservation: No speculation; only safe vehicles
  • No crypto: Cryptocurrency is not allowed in this layer
Your Foundation Layer is your financial shock absorber. Never let it drop below 30% of total portfolio. If it does, the AI Coach will flag this as urgent.

Growth Layer (40%)

Purpose

Long-term wealth compounding through diversified risk assets. This is your 30-year compounding engine.

Target Allocation

40% of your total portfolio value.

Suitable Holdings

  • Large-cap index funds (SPY, VTI, VOO)
  • International equities (VXUS, EFA, IEMG)
  • Dividend stocks (solid, mature companies)
  • Bonds (BND, AGG, individual Treasuries)
  • Real estate investment trusts (VNQ, REITs)
  • Balanced funds (60/40 stock/bond mix or similar)

Rules

  • Diversification required: No single holding should exceed 15% of this layer
  • Long-term focus: Intended for 10+ year holding periods
  • Moderate volatility: Expected annual volatility 8–15%
  • Rebalancing discipline: Rebalance when drift exceeds 5% from target
The Growth Layer is your core wealth. It should be boring, diversified, and left alone. Don't day-trade this layer. The AI Coach will discourage frequent trading here.

Asymmetric Layer (20%)

Purpose

High-risk, high-upside bets where you accept large loss potential for significant upside. Think venture, crypto, thematic bets.

Target Allocation

20% of your total portfolio value.

Suitable Holdings

  • Bitcoin, Ethereum, alt cryptocurrencies
  • Emerging market stocks (high growth, high volatility)
  • Biotech and small-cap growth stocks
  • Call options (if experienced)
  • Venture-backed equity positions
  • Thematic ETFs (AI, cleantech, disruption)

Rules

  • High risk accepted: These positions can lose 50%+ or gain 5x+
  • Position sizing: No single position should exceed 10% of this layer
  • Conviction required: The AI Coach will ask "why" before you add to Asymmetric
  • Rebalancing caps: Rebalance when drift exceeds 3% from target
  • Downside capped: Only risk what you can afford to lose completely
The Asymmetric Layer is where losses happen. A 50% crash here means only a 10% hit to total portfolio (20% × 50%). That's the point: defined downside, unlimited upside, small allocation.

Layer Target Rules & Enforcement

Wealth-OS enforces layer discipline through automated checks and the AI Coach:

Condition Status Action
All layers within 3% of target ✓ Healthy None required; maintain current allocation
Any layer 3–5% off target ⚠ Watch Plan rebalancing with next contribution
Any layer >5% off target 🔴 Urgent Rebalance immediately or discuss with Coach
Foundation Layer <30% 🔴 Critical Stop new Asymmetric/Growth additions immediately
When your layers drift, the Coach will not prevent you from trading, but will ask clarifying questions and remind you of your targets. The system is advisory, not restrictive.

A Practical Example

Scenario: You have a $100,000 portfolio with $40k Foundation, $40k Growth, $20k Asymmetric. You want to add $10,000.

Current state: Perfectly on target (40/40/20).

New allocation: Add $4,000 Foundation, $4,000 Growth, $2,000 Asymmetric → New portfolio: $104,000 with allocation 42.3%, 42.3%, 19.2% (within healthy range).

Another scenario: Market crash hits. Your Growth and Asymmetric layers plunge, but Foundation stays stable. New portfolio: $70,000 with allocation 57% Foundation, 27% Growth, 16% Asymmetric.

Coach guidance: "Your Foundation is above target and Growth/Asymmetric are below. Should we move some Foundation to Growth now that valuations have dropped?" This is when you consider rebalancing.

How Rebalancing Works

Wealth-OS uses a new money first rebalancing approach:

  1. You have new capital to invest (from salary, bonus, savings)
  2. You enter the amount in the Rebalance tab
  3. Wealth-OS calculates how much should go to each layer to bring them back to target
  4. You review the allocation and follow the suggested purchases
  5. You log the trades in the Contributions tab

Key point: This is rebalancing by addition, not forced selling. You never have to sell positions to rebalance unless you choose to.

A Practical Rebalancing Walkthrough

Your situation: You have $100,000 in your portfolio, currently allocated 45% Foundation, 35% Growth, 20% Asymmetric. You just received a $10,000 bonus and want to rebalance.

Rebalancer recommendation:

  • Add $0 to Foundation (already above target)
  • Add $6,500 to Growth (bring it toward 40% target)
  • Add $3,500 to Asymmetric (maintain relative positioning)

After allocation: New portfolio value is $110,000 with allocation 40.9% Foundation, 40.0% Growth, 19.1% Asymmetric (back in balance).

Use the "Preview Allocation" button to see your new portfolio composition before you commit to buying. You can adjust the allocation manually if you want to override the recommendation.

When to Rebalance Manually

Sometimes markets move so sharply that you might want to rebalance by selling, not just buying:

  • Growth Layer crashed 20%: Fell from 40% to 30% of portfolio. You might sell some Foundation and buy Growth at depressed prices.
  • Asymmetric Layer surged 50%: Rose from 20% to 28% of portfolio. You might take profits and redeploy to Foundation/Growth.
  • Foundation Layer below 30%: This is critical. You should sell Growth/Asymmetric and rebuild Foundation immediately.

Discuss these decisions with the AI Coach before executing.

Understanding Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)

DCA is investing a fixed amount at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly, annual) regardless of market price.

Why DCA Works

  • Removes timing risk: You don't have to guess when to invest; you just invest on schedule
  • Reduces average cost: When prices are high, you buy fewer shares. When prices are low, you buy more shares. Over time, this lowers your average cost per share
  • Reduces emotion: A systematic plan removes the temptation to skip investments when markets are scary

DCA in Wealth-OS

You can set up automated DCA by logging regular contributions (e.g., every month) with the Rebalance tab as your guide for allocation.

Example: You get paid $5,000/month. Every month, you log a $5,000 contribution, then use the Rebalancer to allocate it across layers, then you place the trades. This is systematic DCA.

What is the Projector?

The Projector is a multi-scenario growth calculator that forecasts your portfolio value 30 years into the future under three different market assumptions.

Key inputs:

  • Your current portfolio value (loaded automatically)
  • Monthly contribution amount (you set)
  • Projection horizon in years (you set; 1–50 years)
  • Three market return scenarios (pre-configured)

The Three Scenarios

Conservative Scenario (6% annual return)

A cautious estimate assuming slower economic growth, higher interest rates, and muted equity returns. This is roughly: 30% bonds, 70% equities, 0% crypto.

Base Scenario (8% annual return)

The "most likely" case assuming historical equity returns and reasonable diversification. This reflects your 40/40/20 layer allocation reasonably well.

Optimistic Scenario (10% annual return)

A bullish case assuming strong economic growth, tech outperformance, and favorable crypto conditions. 60% equities, 20% bonds, 20% crypto upside.

Projections are NOT guaranteed. Markets can crash. Recessions happen. Your actual returns may differ significantly from these forecasts. Use them as planning tools, not predictions.

The AI Behavioral Coach

Your personal wealth advisor. Not a stock picker, but a behavioral guide that enforces rules, asks hard questions, and keeps you disciplined.

What the Coach Does

The AI Coach is designed to:

  • Provide Behavioral Guidance: Help you think through decisions, not tell you what to do
  • Enforce Layer Rules: Prevent you from breaking allocation rules without thinking
  • Ask "Why": Challenge your reasoning before large trades
  • Offer Context: Remind you of market conditions, your goals, and your risk tolerance
  • Flag Mistakes: Warn when you're about to do something inconsistent with your strategy

What the Coach does NOT do:

  • Pick stocks or time the market
  • Provide regulated financial advice
  • Guarantee returns or promise specific outcomes
  • Force you to do anything (all enforcement is advisory)

  • "Should I rebalance this month?" → Coach analyzes layer drift and gives guidance
  • "Markets are down — what do I do?" → Behavioral coaching to stay the course

  • "Am I on track for retirement?" → Retirement planning context
  • "Give me my monthly summary" → Performance review and next steps

Click any button to populate the chat input, or type your own question.

How the Coach Works

The AI Coach leverages:

  • Real Portfolio Data: Your current positions, P&L, health score, and layer allocations
  • Contribution History: Total contributions, monthly cadence, and allocation trends
  • Personal Context: Your age, location, retirement horizon, and investment conviction
  • Portfolio Rules: Your three-layer framework and target allocations

The Coach asks clarifying questions before recommending new trades and enforces your portfolio rules (e.g., "Why do you want to increase asymmetric beyond 15%?").

🤖 Behind the Scenes: Each chat response is generated by Claude Haiku 4.5, an efficient language model optimized for speed and cost. Your chat history is stored locally in your browser and not logged remotely.

How Conversations Work

  1. You open the Coach and describe a trade idea or question
  2. The Coach reads your message and your portfolio context
  3. The Coach responds with 3–5 sentences (direct and thoughtful)
  4. If you're about to break a rule, the Coach flags it and asks why
  5. If you have conviction, you can override; the Coach acknowledges and logs your choice
  6. You and the Coach continue the conversation until you're confident in your decision

Important: The Coach never prevents you from trading. It only advises and flags violations. You have full control and can override any recommendation.

Coach Personality

The Coach is:

  • Calm: No urgency, no hype, no FOMO
  • Direct: No fluff; specific, honest feedback
  • Concise: 3–5 sentences per response, not essays
  • Skeptical of certainty: Acknowledges complexity and unknown risks

Example Coach responses:

  • "You want to add 50% to your Asymmetric Layer, which would push it to 30%. Your rule is 20%. Before we go further, why is this conviction strong enough to break your allocation?"
  • "That's a good observation about the market. But remember, your Growth Layer is already 42% of your portfolio. Adding more equities increases your overall volatility. Have you considered a different approach?"
  • "You've added $8,000 to the Asymmetric Layer this month. That's your conviction talking, and I respect it. Just make sure you're not overconcentrating in one bet."

Layer Rules Enforcement

The Coach will warn you if you're about to violate these rules:

Rule Limit Coach Action
Foundation never below 30% Hard floor Coach blocks; requires explicit override with reasoning
Layer drift above 5% Urgency threshold Coach flags as urgent; recommends rebalancing
Asymmetric single position >10% Concentration limit Coach warns before you buy; asks if you want to split the position
Trading frequency >3x/month Overtrading check Coach asks if you're chasing momentum; reminds you of long-term focus

Conviction-Based Trading

The Coach uses a conviction framework to assess your readiness to trade:

Before you buy anything outside of your rebalancing plan, the Coach asks:
  1. "Why are you adding to this position NOW versus later?"
  2. "What would have to change for you to sell this?"
  3. "How confident are you? (1–10)"
  4. "How much of your portfolio is this? (% of layer)"

This forces you to articulate your reasoning. Vague answers trigger skepticism from the Coach. Clear answers build confidence.

Pre-populated Prompt Suggestions

The Coach offers quick-start prompts for common conversations:

  • "Should I rebalance now?"
  • "I want to buy [asset]. Is this a good idea?"
  • "My portfolio is down 10%. Should I panic-sell?"
  • "I got a windfall. How should I deploy it?"
  • "Am I overconcentrated in [sector]?"
  • "What should my allocation be for someone my age?"

Click any prompt to populate the chat input and start the conversation.

  • Export the full transcript to a file

Re-read your past conversations with the Coach every quarter. It's humbling to see how your thinking has evolved, and it reinforces the lessons you've learned.

Settings & Data Management

Configure your API keys, manage data persistence, and understand how Wealth-OS stores your information.

API Configuration

To connect Wealth-OS to your Alpaca account:

  1. Go to Settings → API Configuration
  2. Log into your Alpaca account at alpaca.markets
  3. Navigate to Account → Integrations → View API Keys
  4. Copy your API Key (public key)
  5. Copy your Secret Key (private key; treat like a password)
  6. In Wealth-OS Settings, paste both keys
  7. Click "Test Connection"
  8. If successful, your account data will load automatically
Your Secret Key is sensitive. Never share it, never paste it into untrusted websites, and never commit it to version control. If you accidentally expose your keys, regenerate them immediately in your Alpaca dashboard.

Data Persistence (localStorage)

Wealth-OS stores all your personal data locally in your browser's localStorage. This includes:

  • Portfolio holdings and trade history
  • Contribution records
  • AI Coach chat history
  • Preference settings (dark mode, etc.)
  • Rebalancing history

Advantages:

  • Privacy: Your data never leaves your device or browser
  • Offline: You can use Wealth-OS even without internet (limited functionality)
  • Control: You own your data; no one else has access

Disadvantages:

  • No backup: If you clear your browser data or cookies, all Wealth-OS data is deleted
  • Single device: Your data is tied to this browser; you can't access it from another device
  • Sync: If you use multiple browsers or devices, data won't sync across them

Backing Up Your Data

To protect your records, regularly export your data:

  1. Go to Settings → Export Data
  2. Choose what to export (portfolio history, contributions, chat history, all)
  3. Click "Export to JSON"
  4. A file named "wealth-os-backup-[date].json" downloads
  5. Save this file to a secure location (cloud backup, external drive, etc.)

Restoring Data

If you lose your local data, you can restore from a backup:

  1. Go to Settings → Import Data
  2. Click "Choose File" and select your backup JSON
  3. Review the preview of what will be imported
  4. Click "Restore"
  5. Your data is restored to localStorage
Restoring data will overwrite your current localStorage. If you have unsaved changes, export them first.

Currency Handling

Wealth-OS operates in USD. All portfolio values, contributions, and projections are in US dollars.

Multi-currency support is planned for future versions. For now, if you invest in assets denominated in other currencies (GBP, EUR, etc.), convert the values to USD before entering them into Wealth-OS.

Clearing Data

To clear all Wealth-OS data and start fresh:

  1. Go to Settings → Data Management
  2. Click "Clear All Data"
  3. Confirm the deletion (this is permanent)
  4. All local Wealth-OS data is deleted
  5. The app resets to initial state
This action is permanent and cannot be undone. Export your data first if you want to keep any records.
