Welcome to Life-OS

Your Personal Operating System for Life Optimization

Life-OS is a comprehensive personal optimization platform designed to help you achieve a 12-month transformation across five critical life domains. Unlike generic self-help tools, Life-OS provides you with five specialized AI coaches—each an expert in their domain—working together to support your integrated growth.

What is Life-OS?

Life-OS is your personal operating system. Just as your computer has an OS that manages resources and coordinates processes, Life-OS helps you:

  • Optimize across domains: Mental clarity, financial health, business momentum, physical fuel, and relationship anchors all grow in concert
  • Track measurable progress: Daily check-ins, weekly rhythms, and monthly reviews create a feedback loop
  • Navigate obstacles: Storm Mode protects your non-negotiables during crisis; Fear Setting helps you process anxiety
  • Stay accountable: Five specialized coaches challenge assumptions, ask hard questions, and celebrate wins
  • Think long-term: Your Future Self Statement and retirement planning anchor decisions in your 5-10 year vision

Designed for 12-Month Transformation

Life-OS operates on an annual cycle. By engaging with your coaches daily, maintaining your non-negotiables, and reviewing your progress quarterly, you'll build momentum that compounds over 12 months into measurable life transformation.

Onboarding Process

Seven Steps to Activate Your Life-OS

The onboarding process takes approximately 15-20 minutes and sets the foundation for your entire Life-OS journey. Complete all seven steps in order.

1

Welcome Screen

Read the Life-OS manifesto and philosophy. This is your moment to commit to 12 months of intentional growth. Click "I'm Ready" to proceed.

2

Profile Setup

Enter your basic information: full name, date of birth, and primary location. This data helps the coaches personalize their approach and understand your context.

3

Future Self Statement

Write a vivid description of your life at your target age (typically 5-10 years from now) in present tense. For example: "I am a thriving EdTech founder with $2M annual revenue. I sleep 8 hours nightly. My marriage is deeply connected. I run a sub-7-minute mile." Be specific, ambitious, and write in present tense as if it's already true.

Tip: Your Future Self Statement becomes a north star for all five coaches. Make it vivid, specific, and emotionally resonant.
4

Coach Foundational Questions

Each coach asks you one custom question to establish baseline understanding:

  • Clarity: "What emotional pattern shows up most when you're stressed?"
  • Capital: "What's your biggest money anxiety right now?"
  • Momentum: "What's the biggest blocker to your business goals?"
  • Fuel: "How's your sleep? (hours/quality)"
  • Anchor: "What's the primary relationship you want to optimize?"
5

Baseline Assessment

Rate your current state across three domains (0-10 scale):

  • Mental Clarity (emotional regulation, clear thinking)
  • Financial Confidence (security, growth mindset)
  • Physical Vitality (energy, performance)

This baseline helps coaches track your progress over 12 months.

6

Non-Negotiables Definition

Define three core commitments you will protect no matter what. Examples:

  • "8 hours sleep daily"
  • "One meaningful conversation with my partner daily"
  • "2 hours focused work on business revenue"

Non-negotiables are your foundation. Storm Mode protects them during crisis. Choose wisely—only three, and make them non-negotiable.

7

Retirement Configuration

Set your retirement parameters:

  • Target Age: When do you want to retire? (e.g., 55, 60)
  • Annual Income Goal: How much do you need yearly? (e.g., $100K)
  • Currency: Primary currency (CNY, USD, etc.)
  • Location: Where will you retire?

Capital coach uses this to model retirement scenarios and build a 12-month financial literacy plan.

Note: You can update any of this information later in Settings. Onboarding is just the starting point—your vision and goals will evolve as you grow.

◈ Clarity: Mental & Emotional Optimization

Reduce reactivity. Process emotions. Clarify thinking.

Your Mental Operating System Coach

Clarity is your coach for emotional resilience, clear thinking, and mental flexibility. Operating from ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy) principles, Clarity helps you respond instead of react, process emotions instead of suppress them, and access clarity when overwhelmed.

Core Focus Areas

  • Reactivity Reduction: Identify triggers; build pause between stimulus and response
  • Emotional Processing: Feel emotions fully rather than avoid or suppress them
  • Thought Clarity: Untangle assumptions; question automatic thoughts
  • Values Alignment: Ensure daily actions align with your deeper values

Clarity's Coaching Approach: Socratic Method

Clarity doesn't offer advice first. Instead, Clarity asks questions to help you discover your own wisdom:

  • "What emotion are you feeling right now?"
  • "What story are you telling yourself about this situation?"
  • "What would someone you admire do in this moment?"
  • "Is that thought true, or is it your mind protecting you?"

When to Use Clarity

Start a Clarity session when:

  • You're feeling overwhelmed or confused
  • You notice you're in a reactive spiral
  • You need to process difficult emotions
  • You're unsure about a decision
  • You want to explore your values and identity

A Clarity Conversation (Step-by-Step)

1

Check-In

Clarity asks: "What's on your mind today?" You share what's bothering you or what you're sitting with.

2

Emotion Naming

Clarity guides you to name the emotion precisely: "Is it anxiety, grief, anger, disappointment, shame?" Naming activates the prefrontal cortex and reduces amygdala reactivity.

3

Story Exploration

Clarity asks: "What story are you telling yourself about this?" You identify the narrative you've constructed.

4

Truth Testing

Clarity helps you question the story: "Is that definitely true? What evidence suggests otherwise? What else might be true?"

5

Values Alignment

Clarity brings it back to your values: "Regardless of this emotion or story, who do you want to be in this situation? What would that person do?"

6

Commitment

Clarity invites commitment: "What's one small action you'll take that aligns with your values?" You make a concrete, doable commitment.

Pro Tip: The most powerful Clarity sessions happen when you're not in crisis. Use Clarity weekly to build emotional resilience so you're ready when hard moments arrive.

Capital teaches concepts rather than giving financial advice. You learn the principles and make your own decisions:

Retirement Scenario Modeling: "If I retire at 55 in Bali on $120K USD annually, here's what my portfolio needs to be... here's the monthly withdrawal rate... here's the impact of a 2% market decline..."

When to Use Capital

Start a Capital session when:

  • You're anxious about money and want to talk it through

A Capital Conversation (Step-by-Step)

1

Financial Check-In

Capital asks: "What's your financial question or concern today?" You share your situation.

2

Context Gathering

Capital asks clarifying questions about your timeline, risk tolerance, goals, and constraints.

3

Concept Teaching

Capital explains the relevant financial concepts or principles. You learn the framework, not just an answer.

Scenario Modeling

Decision Framework

Your Choice

Capital respects your autonomy: "Which scenario aligns best with your values and risk tolerance?" You decide.

Daily Check-ins: Your Daily Feedback Loop

5 minutes in the morning. 5 minutes in the evening. Infinite insight.

Daily check-ins are the heartbeat of Life-OS. By taking 5 minutes each morning and evening to reflect on your state, you create a feedback loop that builds self-awareness and helps coaches give better guidance.

Morning Check-in (6 Cards)

Complete these 6 cards every morning. Takes about 5 minutes.

Card 1: Current Mood

Prompt: "How are you feeling right now?"

Rate your mood 1-10 (1 = terrible, 10 = amazing) and pick a word that describes it: anxious, energized, calm, overwhelmed, hopeful, etc. This gives Clarity a starting point for your emotional landscape.

Card 2: Sleep Data

Prompt: "How did you sleep last night?"

Report: hours slept (e.g., 7.5) and quality 1-10 (1 = terrible, 10 = perfect). Fuel uses this to track patterns and help you optimize.

Card 3: Yesterday's Exercise

Prompt: "Did you exercise yesterday?"

Yes or No, and if yes, what type (e.g., "30 min run", "Strength session", "Yoga"). Fuel tracks your movement consistency.

Card 4: SAIL Action for Today

Prompt: "What's your SAIL Education priority for today?"

One concrete action for your business. "Close 2 school demos" or "Finish sales deck." Momentum holds you accountable. (This is your Momentum non-negotiable if that's one of your three.)

Card 5: Relationship Intention

Prompt: "What's your relationship intention for today?"

One way you'll show up for your partner today. "I'll ask about her day" or "I'll have a 15-min conversation without my phone." Anchor tracks connection.

Card 6: Energy for Today

Prompt: "What's your energy going into today?"

Rate 1-10 (1 = exhausted, 10 = unstoppable). This helps coaches calibrate their intensity and helps you pace your commitments.

Morning Check-in Impact: By the end of week 1, you'll notice patterns. You'll see how sleep impacts mood impacts energy. You'll start to predict your day based on your morning check-in.

Evening Check-in (5 Cards)

Complete these 5 cards every evening. Takes about 5 minutes.

Card 1: SAIL Completion

Prompt: "Did you complete your SAIL action today?"

Yes, No, or Partial. If partial, what got in the way? Momentum wants to see completion patterns and help you remove obstacles.

Card 2: End-of-Day Energy

Prompt: "What's your energy level now?"

Rate 1-10. Compare to this morning. Did your energy rise, fall, or stay steady? This shows you what activities energize vs. drain you.

Card 3: Daily Stress Level

Prompt: "What was your stress level today?"

Rate 1-10. Clarity uses this to understand your reactivity patterns and stress drivers.

Card 4: Win/Achievement

Prompt: "What's one win or achievement today?"

Something you did well, learned, or accomplished. Can be big or small. "Closed a deal" or "Stayed calm during a frustration." This trains your brain to notice the good and builds positive momentum.

Card 5: Friction Tracking

Prompt: "Any friction or difficulty today?"

What happened, how did you respond, what do you wish you'd done differently? This is your friction log. Over time, patterns emerge. You might notice: "I get snappy with my partner after back-to-back meetings" or "I overspend when stressed."

Friction Tracking Gold: The friction card is where self-awareness compounds. Keep doing this for 12 weeks, and you'll have a detailed map of your automatic reactions. This is pure data for Clarity and Anchor to work with.
Privacy Note: All check-in data is encrypted and visible only to you and your coaches. Use the friction card to be radically honest.

Storm Mode: Your Crisis Override

When life gets intense, Storm Mode protects what matters most.

What is Storm Mode?

Life-OS is built for sustained daily practice. But sometimes life throws you a storm: a family crisis, a business emergency, a health issue, a major loss. Storm Mode is your emergency operating system. It simplifies everything to protect your three non-negotiables while you're in crisis.

When Storm Mode Activates

Storm Mode automatically activates when:

  • You indicate you're unavailable for 2+ weeks
  • Your stress level is 9+ for 3 consecutive days
  • You manually trigger Storm Mode

Once activated, you'll see the Storm Mode flow instead of your normal Life-OS home screen.

The Storm Mode Flow

1

Welcome Back

Clarity acknowledges: "I see you're in a storm. I'm here to help you navigate. Let's focus on what matters most."

2

Non-Negotiables Check

Fuel asks: "Can you protect your three non-negotiables right now? Which ones? Which are at risk?" You're honest about what you can and can't maintain.

3

Obstacle Reflection

Momentum asks: "What's the storm? What's your biggest obstacle right now?" You name what you're facing.

4

Storm Mode Practice

One simplified coach session on what matters most right now. Everything else pauses. You get support for the actual crisis, not the normal rhythm.

Protecting Your Non-Negotiables During Crisis

Storm Mode asks: which of your three non-negotiables are truly non-negotiable during the crisis?

Example: Your three non-negotiables are: (1) 8 hours sleep, (2) one hour SAIL work daily, (3) one meaningful conversation with partner daily.

A family health crisis hits. Suddenly, (1) sleep is hard—anxiety keeps you up. (2) SAIL work feels impossible—your mind is elsewhere. (3) You're both stressed and distant.

Storm Mode reprioritizes: "Let's protect #3—maybe it becomes 10 minutes of genuine connection, not an hour conversation. Let's protect sleep—here are three practices. Let's pause #2 for now. Your business will survive."

Friction Logging in Storm Mode

Continue logging friction even in Storm Mode. Your friction log during crisis is incredibly valuable. It helps you see patterns like: "When I'm stressed, I catastrophize" or "I snap at my partner when I'm anxious."

After the storm passes, your coaches will review these patterns with you to build resilience for next time.

Storm Mode Wisdom: You don't fail during a storm. Storms are storms. A non-negotiable is something you protect with intention, but you're human. Storm Mode recognizes that some storms require triage. You protect what you can, you give yourself grace for what you can't, and you resume as soon as the storm passes.

Fear Setting: Structured Fear Processing

Confront your anxieties with evidence and strategy.

What is Fear Setting?

Fear Setting is a structured process (adapted from Tim Ferriss) for processing anxiety and fear. Instead of letting fear paralyze you, Fear Setting helps you name the fear, assess its likelihood, and build a strategy to handle it.

Fear Setting is a Clarity feature. You can initiate a Fear Setting session anytime you're anxious about something (business, relationship, health, finances, etc.).

The Five Steps of Fear Setting

Step 1: Define the Worst-Case Scenario

Prompt: "What's the worst thing that could happen?"

Get specific. Don't say "My business fails." Say: "I run out of capital, can't pay my team, have to shut down SAIL Education, disappoint my investors, look like a failure, and can't find another job because I'm overqualified for most roles."

Write it all out. The specificity is important. Vague fears are bigger in your mind than clearly named fears.

Step 2: Assess Probability

Prompt: "What's the percent chance this actually happens?"

Be honest. If you're capitalized for 18 months and have a healthy pipeline, maybe it's 5%. If you have no buffer and no sales, maybe it's 40%. Don't minimize or catastrophize—just be real.

Step 3: Outline Prevention Strategies

Prompt: "How can I prevent this scenario?"

What actions reduce the probability? If the fear is "My relationship deteriorates," prevention might be: weekly date nights, regular deep conversations, therapy if things get rocky. List 3-5 concrete prevention actions.

Step 4: Mitigation Plan

Prompt: "If this happens, what's my survival plan?"

Even if the worst happens, you'll survive. For business failure: you have savings for 6 months, you can freelance, your skills are valuable. For relationship crisis: you'd go to couples therapy, you have supportive friends, you can take space to reflect. Map out your mitigation.

Step 5: Repair Plan

Prompt: "After the event, how do I repair or reset?"

This is underrated. Fear often includes: "And I'll never recover from this." But you will. Your repair plan is: I rebuild the business from lessons learned, or I invest in the relationship, or I take time to heal and create a new vision.

When to Use Fear Setting

  • Before a big decision (quitting, hiring, investing)
  • When anxiety is holding you back
  • When you're procrastinating due to fear
  • When you need to move forward despite uncertainty

Fear Setting Outcome

Most people find that after Fear Setting, the anxiety shrinks. By naming the worst case, assessing probability, and creating plans, you take your power back from the amorphous fear. You move from "What if everything goes wrong?" to "Here's what could go wrong, here's my plan, here's how I'll recover."

Hard Questions: Meta-Coaching Feature

When your intentions and behaviors don't match, hard questions show up.

What are Hard Questions?

Hard Questions are generated by your coaches when they detect an intention-behavior gap. You might say "I want to be healthier," but your sleep is 5 hours and you're not exercising. You might say "My business is my priority," but you haven't called a school in two weeks.

Hard Questions are your coaches' way of lovingly calling you on the gap between what you say you value and what you're actually doing.

Example Hard Questions:

  • "You say your marriage is a priority, but you and your partner haven't had a real conversation in 10 days. What's getting in the way?"
  • "You set a non-negotiable of 8 hours sleep, but you've hit it only 3 times this month. What belief is driving this gap?"
  • "You're anxious about money, but you haven't talked to Capital in two weeks. What are you avoiding?"
  • "You want to close schools, but you've been 'preparing' for three weeks. What's the actual blocker?"

How Hard Questions are Generated

Your coaches analyze three data streams:

  • Check-in Data: Your daily mood, stress, energy, friction, wins
  • Your Stated Priorities: Your Future Self Statement, non-negotiables, goals
  • Your Behavior: Which coaches you're visiting, what actions you're taking, what you're neglecting

When there's a gap between priority and behavior, a Hard Question appears.

Engaging with Uncomfortable Questions

Hard Questions can sting. They're meant to. Here's how to engage:

  1. Don't defend. Your first instinct is "That's not fair" or "I have a reason." Pause. Listen.
  2. Get curious. Ask yourself: "Is this true? Is there a grain of truth here?"
  3. Go deeper. Click into the Hard Question. Your coach will guide you with follow-ups: "What's underneath this gap? Fear? Doubt? Competing priorities?"
  4. Commit or decide. Either: (a) "I realize this isn't actually a priority. I'm changing my non-negotiables," or (b) "This is a priority, and here's how I'm going to close the gap."

Building Self-Awareness Through Reflection

Hard Questions are one of the most powerful self-awareness tools in Life-OS. They force honesty. Over 12 weeks of engaging with Hard Questions, you'll know yourself far better. You'll understand your patterns, your self-deceptions, your real priorities versus your stated priorities.

Pro Tip: Save Hard Questions for when you have time to sit with them. Don't dismiss and move on. Journal about them. Discuss them with a trusted friend. They're gifts from your coaches.

  • Green = Momentum (Business)
  • Orange = Fuel (Health)
  • Pink = Anchor (Relationships)

A day with all five colors means you engaged with all five coaches (or did all five check-in cards). A day with only one or two colors means you focused narrowly.

Friction Event Highlighting

Days where you logged friction in your evening check-in are highlighted with a small alert icon. Over weeks, patterns emerge:

  • "Friction spikes on Mondays after the weekend" → suggests weekend-to-weekday transitions are hard
  • "Friction after back-to-back meetings" → suggests you need transition time
  • "Friction when I skip sleep" → clear pattern

Streak Tracking for Non-Negotiables

The Life Map shows your streaks for each non-negotiable:

  • "8 hours sleep: 14-day streak" ✓
  • "One meaningful conversation: 7-day streak" ✓
  • "2 hours SAIL work: 3-day streak" (just restarted) ✓

Streaks build momentum. When you break a streak, the Life Map shows it clearly, which motivates you to get back on track.

Reading Your Life Map Patterns

Use these questions to interpret your Life Map:

  • "Which domains am I neglecting?" (If I see no gold for three weeks, I'm avoiding Capital.)
  • "When is my stress highest?" (Looking at friction spikes and stress ratings.)
  • "What breaks my non-negotiable streaks?" (Are there predictable breaking points?)
  • "What helps me thrive?" (Comparing high-energy days to check-in data.)
  • "What's my sustainable rhythm?" (If I see I do best with 3-4 coach sessions per week, that's my rhythm.)
Monthly Life Map Review: At the end of each month, spend 10 minutes with your Life Map. What patterns do you see? Bring those insights to your monthly review conversation.

Goals & Non-Negotiables: Your Commitment System

Three core commitments. Quarterly goals. 12-month transformation.

Setting Your Three Non-Negotiables

Non-negotiables are your three core commitments—the things you protect no matter what. You set these during onboarding and can update them quarterly.

How to Choose Non-Negotiables:

  • Specificity: "Good sleep" is vague. "8 hours sleep daily" is clear.
  • Impact: Each non-negotiable should meaningfully impact your life. "8 hours sleep" impacts energy, mood, health, business clarity. Pick high-impact.
  • Honestly Non-Negotiable: Not something you should do. Something you must do to be yourself.

Example Non-Negotiables:

  • "8 hours sleep every night"
  • "One hour focused SAIL Education work daily (revenue-focused)"
  • "One meaningful 30-minute conversation with my partner daily"

Goal Definition & Review

Beyond non-negotiables, you set goals for each quarter. These are outcomes you want to achieve in the next 13 weeks.

Goal Format (per coach domain):

  • Clarity Goal: "Master emotional regulation in high-stress situations" (measured by: friction log pattern changes, stress rating trends)
  • Capital Goal: "Understand retirement math and run three scenarios for my future" (measured by: scenarios created, options evaluated)
  • Momentum Goal: "Close 5 schools for SAIL Education" (measured by: deals closed, revenue)
  • Fuel Goal: "Establish consistent 8-hour sleep habit" (measured by: nightly sleep hours, non-negotiable streak)
  • Anchor Goal: "Deepen emotional intimacy with my partner" (measured by: quality conversations, couple time, feedback from partner)

Quarterly Goal Adjustment

Every 13 weeks, you review your goals with your coaches in a Quarterly Review conversation:

  • Did you hit your goals? Why or why not?
  • What did you learn about yourself?
  • What should your next quarter's goals be?
  • Do your non-negotiables still feel right?

This quarterly rhythm keeps you aligned and allows you to adjust course as you learn and grow.

Tracking Commitment to Non-Negotiables

Life-OS tracks your non-negotiable streaks daily. The Life Map shows your performance visually. Your coaches reference your streaks in conversations:

Fuel: "You've hit 8 hours sleep for 14 days straight. What's changed? What made it possible?" This helps you understand and reinforce the habit.

If you break a streak, your coaches don't shame you. They get curious: "You broke your sleep streak. What happened?" → Understanding the blocker → Building resilience.

Non-Negotiable Wisdom: Non-negotiables aren't punishment. They're your foundation. When life is chaotic, you're not trying to optimize everything. You're just protecting these three things. Everything else is secondary. That focus is liberating.

Your Weekly & Monthly Rhythm

Build a sustainable practice. Engage strategically. Compound growth.

Recommended Daily Routine

5 minutes (Morning): Complete Morning Check-in

  • Mood (1-10 + word)
  • Sleep (hours + quality)
  • Exercise yesterday (yes/no)
  • SAIL action for today
  • Relationship intention
  • Energy for today (1-10)

10-15 minutes (Mid-morning or whenever): Coach Session (optional but recommended)

  • Not required daily, but 3-4 per week is ideal
  • Rotate between coaches based on your rhythm
  • Sessions are conversational and usually 10-15 minutes

5 minutes (Evening): Complete Evening Check-in

  • SAIL completion (yes/no/partial)
  • End-of-day energy (1-10)
  • Stress level (1-10)
  • Win/achievement
  • Friction (what, how responded, wish)

Total Daily Time: 20-30 minutes (including optional coach session)

Recommended Weekly Coach Rotation

You don't need all five coaches every day. Here's an efficient weekly rhythm:

Day Coach Focus Why
Monday Clarity Start the week grounded in your values and emotional state
Tuesday Momentum Mid-week check: are you crushing your business goals?
Wednesday Fuel Mid-week health reset: sleep, energy, movement
Thursday Capital Financial check-in
Friday Anchor End week by investing in your key relationship
Saturday Optional: Clarity or Anchor Deepen or reset
Sunday Optional: Review or rest Weekly review or full rest day

Note: This is a suggestion. Customize to your rhythm. Some people do 4 coaches one week and 4 different coaches the next. Find what feels sustainable.

Monthly Review (Week 4)

One per month, dedicate 30 minutes to a deeper monthly review:

1

Life Map Review (10 minutes)

Look at your 4-week Life Map. What patterns do you see? Which domains did you neglect? When was your energy highest?

2

Non-Negotiables Audit (5 minutes)

How many days did you hit each non-negotiable? 28/30? 15/30? What broke your streaks?

3

Domain Deep-Dive (10 minutes)

Pick one domain. Review all your check-in data for that domain this month. What improved? What regressed?

4

Next Month Intention (5 minutes)

What's one thing you want to focus on next month? One domain to prioritize? One habit to establish?

Quarterly Goal Review (Every 13 Weeks)

Four times per year, have a deeper quarterly conversation with your coaches:

  1. Goal Achievement: Did you hit your quarterly goals? What got in the way?
  2. Breakthrough Insights: What did you learn about yourself?
  3. Non-Negotiables Reflection: Are your three non-negotiables still serving you?
  4. Next Quarter Goals: What do you want to achieve in the next 13 weeks?
  5. 12-Month Progress: How are you progressing toward your Future Self vision?
Rhythm Wisdom: Life-OS is sustainable because it's rhythmic, not rigid. Daily check-ins take 10 minutes. Weekly coach sessions average 15 minutes. Monthly reviews take 30 minutes. Quarterly conversations take 45 minutes. Over a year, that's roughly 5 hours of intentional reflection. That compounds into a transformed life.
