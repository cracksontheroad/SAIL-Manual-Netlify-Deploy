SuperStudent Manual

Your comprehensive guide to mastering the AI-powered student companion for self-directed learning.

Welcome to Your Learning Companion
SuperStudent is an intelligent, AI-powered platform designed specifically for self-directed learners. Whether you're managing coursework, preparing for exams, or tracking your academic progress, SuperStudent empowers you with tools that adapt to your learning style.

What is SuperStudent?

Overview

SuperStudent is a standalone student companion application built for modern learners who want to take control of their education. Unlike traditional learning platforms, SuperStudent combines course management, AI-powered tutoring, progress tracking, and wellbeing monitoring in a single, intuitive interface.

Core Purpose

SuperStudent helps you:

Manage courses, assignments, and deadlines in one place
Generate practice assignments and study materials on-demand using AI
Learn in your preferred style with 12+ AI-powered teaching methods
Track grades, study time, and academic progress in real-time
Maintain mental and emotional wellbeing with daily mood check-ins
Build a digital portfolio showcasing your achievements
Collaborate safely with teachers and peers

Who Should Use SuperStudent?

SuperStudent is designed for:

  • Self-Directed Learners: Students managing their own learning pace and goals
  • Online Learners: Those in fully remote or hybrid learning environments
  • Students Seeking Extra Support: Learners who benefit from AI tutoring and personalized practice
  • All Ages & Grades: From middle school through university and beyond

Setting Up Your Profile

Your first time using SuperStudent? Follow these steps to create your profile and personalize your learning experience.

First-Time Setup (Step-by-Step)

1

Enter Your Name

Start by entering your full name. This will appear on your profile, progress reports, and portfolio. You can update this anytime.

2

Select Your Grade Level

Choose your current grade (e.g., 9th Grade, 10th Grade, College Year 1, etc.). This helps SuperStudent tailor content difficulty and recommendations.

3

Add Your Subjects

List the subjects you're currently studying (e.g., Mathematics, History, Biology, English). You can add or remove subjects as needed.

4

Define Your Study Goals

Set 1-3 primary goals (e.g., "Improve math grades by 10%", "Complete all assignments on time", "Master essay writing"). These goals will drive your daily dashboard.

5

Choose Your Learning Preferences

Indicate your preferred learning styles (visual, auditory, hands-on, etc.). SuperStudent will use these to recommend the best AI tutoring methods.

Tip: You can return to setup and update your preferences anytime from Settings. Your learning style preferences may change as you discover what works best for you!

Rubrics & Assessment Criteria

Understanding rubrics is crucial for academic success. When viewing a rubric:

  • Criteria: The areas being assessed (e.g., "Critical Thinking", "Organization")
  • Performance Levels: Usually ranging from "Emerging" to "Excellent"
  • Point Values: Points awarded for each performance level
  • Descriptions: What each level looks like in practice
AI Rubric Analysis: Click "Analyze with AI" on any rubric to get personalized suggestions for how to meet each criterion. The AI Tutor will break down what's needed at each level and provide tips for reaching the highest score.

Submitting an Assignment (Step-by-Step)

1

Click on the Assignment

Open the assignment card to view full details, rubric, and submission form.

2

Review the Requirements

Read the assignment brief, rubric, and any attached instructions. Click "Learn About This Topic" if you need background information from the AI Tutor.

3

Prepare Your Work

Complete your work in your preferred tool (Microsoft Word, Google Docs, Canva, code editor, etc.). Save your file locally first.

4

AI Task Generator

The AI Task Generator creates custom practice assignments tailored to your current learning needs. Perfect for exam prep, concept mastery, and extra practice.

What is the AI Task Generator?

Instead of searching for practice problems or study materials online, the AI Task Generator creates brand-new, original assignments in seconds. You specify what you want to learn, and the AI generates a complete assignment with a grading rubric.

Generating a Practice Assignment (Step-by-Step)

1

Select a Topic

Enter the specific topic you want to practice (e.g., "photosynthesis", "calculus derivatives", "Shakespearean sonnets", "French subjunctive mood").

2

Choose Your Subject

Select which subject the topic belongs to (Biology, Math, English Literature, French, etc.).

3

Pick an Assignment Type

Choose from Essay, Homework Problem Set, Exam-Style Quiz, or Investigation Project (see types below).

4

Set the Difficulty Level

Choose Foundation (basic), Standard (grade-level appropriate), or Advanced (challenging, extension content).

5

Click Generate

The AI creates your assignment in 30-60 seconds. You'll see the full assignment prompt and grading rubric.

Assignment Types

📝 Essay +

Best for: Analyzing concepts, synthesizing ideas, practicing writing skills.

What you get: An essay prompt with specific requirements (word count, structure, citations), a grading rubric evaluating thesis, evidence, organization, and style.

Time to complete: 30 minutes - 2 hours depending on difficulty.

🧮 Homework +

Best for: Problem-solving, calculations, applying formulas, working through examples.

What you get: A set of 5-15 problems ranging from simple to complex, complete with hints and worked examples for learning.

Time to complete: 20 minutes - 1 hour depending on difficulty.

📋 Exam +

Best for: Test prep, identifying knowledge gaps, timed practice.

What you get: 20-40 multiple-choice, short-answer, and/or essay questions mimicking real exam formats. Includes answer key and explanations.

Time to complete: Timed (usually 1-2 hours). You can take it under test conditions or untimed for learning.

🔬 Investigation +

Best for: Deep exploration of topics, research projects, critical thinking.

What you get: A research question or investigation prompt, scaffolding questions to guide your exploration, and a rubric evaluating inquiry, evidence, and conclusions.

Time to complete: 2-5 hours depending on scope and difficulty.

Difficulty Levels

Level Description When to Use
Foundation Basic concepts, simple examples, straightforward problems Reviewing fundamentals, learning a new topic from scratch
Standard Grade-level appropriate, mixed simple and complex problems Regular practice, typical homework, standard exams
Advanced Challenging problems, synthesis, extension content Preparing for advanced placement, mastery, competition prep

Understanding Generated Rubrics

Every generated assignment includes a detailed rubric. The rubric breaks down:

  • Criteria: What aspects of your work are being evaluated
  • Performance Descriptors: What "excellent", "good", "developing", and "emerging" look like
  • Point Distributions: How many points each criterion is worth
Self-Grade Your Work: After completing a generated assignment, use the rubric to grade your own work. This helps you identify areas for improvement before submitting to your teacher or using the assignment purely for practice.

Saving Generated Assignments

Once you generate an assignment:

  • Click "Save to My Library" to keep it for future reference
  • Print or download as PDF for offline work
  • Share with classmates (the assignment itself, not your answers)
  • Return to it anytime from your Study Library
Tip: Build a personal practice library by generating and saving assignments in your areas of weakness. Review these periodically to track improvement over time.

AI Tutor

The AI Tutor is your intelligent, always-available learning companion. It adapts to YOUR learning style and explains concepts in a way that makes sense to you. Choose from 12 different learning methods, ask follow-up questions, and access your learning history anytime.

Overview of the AI Tutor

The AI Tutor uses advanced language models (powered by Claude, Gemini, and ElevenLabs) to generate personalized explanations, visual content, interactive activities, and audio-visual materials—all tailored to your selected learning style.

Key Features:

Choose from 12 learning methods (visual, auditory, hands-on, etc.)
Ask follow-up questions to deepen understanding
Save and organize all generated content by topic
Review learning history anytime
Multi-language support (18 languages)
Content moderation for safety and accuracy

12 Learning Styles Explained

Each learning style is best suited for different topics and learners. Experiment to find what works for you!

👁️ Visual Learner - Mind Maps & Diagrams +

How it works: The AI generates an interactive mind map showing concepts, relationships, and hierarchies visually.

Best for: Understanding how concepts relate, seeing the "big picture", organizing complex information.

Example: Ask about "The Water Cycle" and get a beautiful, clickable diagram showing evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection.

Output format: Interactive SVG diagram that you can click to expand sections and see details.

👂 Auditory Learner - Spoken-Style Explanations +

How it works: The AI explains concepts as if speaking to you directly, using conversational language, tone, and pacing.

Best for: Learners who prefer listening over reading, getting explanations in natural language.

Example: Ask about "Photosynthesis" and get an explanation like: "Imagine plants are like tiny solar panels. Here's how they work..."

Output format: Text written in conversational, speech-like style you can read or pair with Audio Explainer (🎙️).

📖 Reading/Writing - Detailed Notes & Definitions +

How it works: The AI generates comprehensive notes with key terms, definitions, examples, and citations.

Best for: Learners who love reading, need detailed documentation, prefer structured notes for study.

Example: Ask about "American Revolutionary War" and get organized notes with sections on causes, key figures, major battles, and consequences.

Output format: Formatted text document with headings, bullet points, definitions, and citations.

🤲 Hands-On Learner - Step-by-Step Examples +

How it works: The AI provides worked examples showing every step of a process or problem-solving approach.

Best for: Learners who learn by doing, need to see examples before attempting problems independently.

Example: Ask about "Solving Quadratic Equations" and get 3-5 complete worked examples showing each step, from setup to final answer.

Output format: Step-by-step guide with annotated examples.

🔀 Analogy Thinker - Real-World Comparisons +

How it works: The AI explains concepts using real-world analogies and comparisons you can relate to.

Best for: Abstract concepts, learning through metaphor and comparison.

Example: Ask about "Mitochondria" and get: "Think of mitochondria as the battery factory in your cells. Just like batteries power your devices, mitochondria power your cells with energy."

Output format: Explanation using everyday analogies and comparisons.

⚡ Quick Summary - Bullet-Point Overview +

How it works: The AI distills complex concepts into a concise, scannable bullet-point list.

Best for: Quick review, last-minute studying, getting the essentials without details.

Example: Ask about "Photosynthesis" and get: "• Sunlight → Energy | • Water + CO2 → Glucose | • Happens in chloroplasts | • Process: Light Reactions + Calvin Cycle"

Output format: Short bullet points, ideal for flashcards or cheat sheets.

🎬 Explainer Video - 6-Scene Auto-Animated +

How it works: The AI generates a 2-3 minute animated educational video with narration, showing the concept visually step-by-step.

Best for: Visual learners, understanding processes and sequences, engaging videos for review.

Example: Ask about "Gravity" and get a 2-minute animated video showing objects falling, forces explained visually, with narration and text overlays.

Output format: Downloadable MP4 video (480p or 1080p).

Generation time: 2-3 minutes.

🃏 Flashcard Game - Interactive 10-Card Deck +

How it works: The AI generates 10 interactive flashcards with questions on one side and answers on the other. Includes a spaced-repetition algorithm to help you learn more effectively.

Best for: Memorization, vocabulary, quick facts, making study fun and interactive.

Example: Ask about "Spanish Vocabulary: Foods" and get 10 cards with Spanish food words on one side and English translations on the other.

Features: Flip cards, mark as "got it" or "need more practice", track your progress across sessions.

📊 Infographic - Gemini-Powered Visual Stats +

How it works: The AI generates a visually stunning infographic using Google Gemini's image generation, showing data, statistics, or key points visually.

Best for: Presenting data, understanding statistics, visual learning through beautiful graphics.

Example: Ask about "Climate Change: By the Numbers" and get an infographic with statistics on rising temperatures, sea levels, carbon emissions—all visually represented.

Output format: High-resolution PNG infographic (1200x1600 pixels).

🔀 Flow Diagram - Interactive Process Flow +

How it works: The AI creates an interactive flowchart or process diagram showing how something works step-by-step, with decision points and branching paths.

Best for: Understanding processes, workflows, decision-making paths, scientific processes.

Example: Ask about "How to Write an Essay" and get an interactive flowchart starting from "Choose a Topic" → "Research" → "Outline" → "Write Intro" → etc., with clickable sections.

Output format: Interactive SVG diagram with tooltips on hover.

🎙️ Audio Explainer - Gemini TTS Narration +

How it works: The AI generates an explanation and uses Google Gemini's text-to-speech to create a natural-sounding audio file (like a podcast or audiobook chapter).

Best for: Auditory learners, learning while commuting, getting explanations without reading.

Example: Ask about "The History of the Internet" and get a 5-10 minute audio explanation in a natural voice, available in 18 languages.

Output format: MP3 audio file (playable in-app or downloadable).

Duration: Usually 5-10 minutes depending on topic complexity.

Languages: English, Spanish, French, German, Chinese, Japanese, and 12 more.

🎬 Video Explainer - Gemini Narrated Slideshow +

How it works: The AI generates a slideshow with beautiful visuals and Gemini TTS narration, creating a polished educational video.

Best for: Visual + auditory learners, engaging video content, presentation-quality explanations.

Example: Ask about "The Solar System" and get a 3-5 minute slideshow with images of planets, narrated explanation, and smooth transitions.

Output format: MP4 video with slides and narration.

Duration: Usually 3-7 minutes.

Study Streak Tracking

A "study streak" is the number of consecutive days you've logged study time. The longer your streak, the closer you are to mastery!

  • Current Streak: Days in a row you've studied
  • Longest Streak: Your all-time record
  • Streak Protection: If you miss a day, you can "protect" your streak once per month by making up study time within 48 hours
Tip: Even 30 minutes of focused study counts! You don't need to study for hours to maintain a streak. Consistency is more important than duration.
